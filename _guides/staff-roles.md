# Staff Roles

This page gives a clear overview of staff roles, responsibilities, and where to find more detail.

---

## Quick Reference

| Role | Short Description |
|---|---|
| **Helpers** | Front-line staff who welcome new players, answer common questions, and route issues to the right team. |
| **Moderation** | Handle enforcement, escalations, server settings, and major decisions. |
| **DMs** | Plan and run games, manage bounties/missions, and coordinate rewards. |
| **Game Keeper** | Maintain game systems, review submitted content, and coordinate rewards with DMs. |
| **Event Planners** | Design and run server-wide events; prepare materials and coordinate staff. |
| **Wish DMs & Keepers** | Manage Wish games and the wishlist queue; assign staff to wishes. |
| **NPC Staff** | Provide in-character NPC interactions and support roleplay scenes. |
| **Dragonspeaker** | Technical staff who maintain code, assist with fixes, and implement automation. |
| **Wyrmlings** | Apprentice Dragonspeakers learning the technical side. |

---

## General Staff Expectations

Staff keep the community running. In general:

- **Lead by example** and follow the rules
- **Speak with intent and empathy**; operate in good faith
- **Address hateful or inappropriate content** quickly and escalate as needed

---

## Support and Escalation

- If you are unsure who to contact, ask in the staff channel and ping @Mods depending on severity
- Escalate repeated or serious issues to Mods

## Detailed Role Descriptions

### Helpers

**Full guide**: [Helpers Guide]({{ '/guides/helpers.html' | relative_url }})

Front-line staff who welcome new players, answer common questions, and guide members to the correct channels or staff when needed.

**Responsibilities:**
- Welcome new players and assist with initial setup
- Answer common questions and offer quick help
- Route questions or issues to the right staff role (e.g., Mods, DMs, Game Keeper)
- Keep an eye on RP activity and signal any concerns to Mods

---

### Moderation

Moderators (Mods) enforce server rules and manage moderation tasks. For server settings or major escalations, use the staff channels to alert senior staff or the owner as appropriate.

**Day-to-Day Moderation:**
- Monitor chat and enforce rules
- Give warnings, timeouts, and handle quick moderation actions
- Escalate repeated or serious incidents to senior Mods

**Leadership & Policy:**
- Manage server settings and major rule decisions as a group of staff leads
- Review escalated moderation cases and decide on necessary actions
- Assign roles and ensure staff policies are followed

**Getting Help:**
- If you are unsure who to contact, ping `@Mods` in staff channels for moderation help

---

### DMs (Dungeon Masters)

**Full guide**: [DM Guide]({{ '/guides/dm-info.html' | relative_url }})

DMs run games for the community. They plan, submit, and execute bounties, missions, and other game content.

**Responsibilities:**
- Prep, plan, and submit bounties or missions following server guidelines
- Coordinate player selection and scheduling with other staff
- Run bounties/missions and manage their game channels
- Add and remove roles for their game channels when appropriate
- Report server or player issues that arise during games to staff

**Best Practices:**
- Keep clear notes on player agreements, rewards, and any special rules
- Communicate with Tracker Helpers and Game Keepers as needed
- Stay organized with your game timeline and player expectations

**Additional Resources:**
- [Game Resources]({{ '/guides/game-resources.html' | relative_url }})
- [Event: Dream Defence]({{ '/guides/events-dream-defence.html' | relative_url }})

---

### Game Keeper

Game Keepers maintain game systems and coordinate rewards and content.

**Responsibilities:**
- Review and approve submitted game content and rewards
- Assist DMs with timing, pacing, and problem-solving during games
- Help finalize rewards and support players in claiming them

**Collaboration:**
- Work closely with DMs and Trackers to ensure events and bounties run smoothly

**Related Resources:**
- [DM Guide]({{ '/guides/dm-info.html' | relative_url }})
- [Game Resources]({{ '/guides/game-resources.html' | relative_url }})

---

### Event Planners

Event Planners brainstorm, plan, prepare, and run server-wide events. They create content (topics, descriptions, images, NPCs, rewards) and coordinate staff to run events.

**Responsibilities:**
- Plan event ideas and produce event materials
- Coordinate with staff and announce events in appropriate channels
- Run events or assign staff to run them

**Related Resources:**
- [Event: Dream Defence]({{ '/guides/events-dream-defence.html' | relative_url }})
- [DM Guide]({{ '/guides/dm-info.html' | relative_url }})

---

### Wish System (Wish DMs & Wish Keepers)

The Wish System handles special Wish games with two related roles.

**Wish DMs:**
- Run Wish games
- Coordinate with players
- Deliver requested content when possible

**Wish Keepers:**
- Gather wish details
- Maintain the queue in the Tracker and on-server
- Assign staff to execute wishes

**Related Resources:**
- [DM Guide]({{ '/guides/dm-info.html' | relative_url }})

---

### NPC Staff

NPC staff provide in-character interactions for players and support roleplay scenes.

**Responsibilities:**
- Maintain minimal weekly availability to be pingable as NPCs
- Roleplay requested NPC interactions (shops, scenes, or storylines)
- Assist players in transactions or scene-related tasks

**Activity Expectations:**
- Try to be available for at least one scene per month to be considered active
- See the NPC guide channel for more specific procedures and resources

**Related Resources:**
- [Staff Rewards]({{ '/guides/staff-rewards.html' | relative_url }})

---

### Dragonspeaker (Technical Staff)

Dragonspeakers maintain code and technical systems used by the server. They are the go-to staff for code questions and fixes.

**Responsibilities:**
- Maintain and fix code used across the server
- Answer questions about code usage and error messages
- Propose and implement new code to streamline server tasks (with Mod approval for major changes)
- Coordinate with other staff to understand technical needs

**Important Notes:**
- Major code changes should be approved by a Mod before deployment

**Related Resources:**
- [Staff Rewards]({{ '/guides/staff-rewards.html' | relative_url }})

---

### Wyrmlings (Apprentice Dragonspeakers)

Wyrmlings are members who want to learn and assist with the technical side but are not yet full Dragonspeakers. This is an apprenticeship-style role.

**Responsibilities:**
- Learn by assisting Dragonspeakers with small tasks and fixes
- Work on supervised, low-risk tasks:
  - Bug fixes
  - Small helper scripts
  - Documentation updates

**Pathway to Dragonspeaker:**
- Wyrmlings who demonstrate reliability, understanding of the codebase, and good communication may be invited to take on more responsibility
- Major responsibility increases require Mod approval

**Getting Involved:**
- Contact a current Dragonspeaker or a Mod if you're interested

## Availability & Expectations

This section explains how availability is tracked and what to do if you need to step away.

### Availability rules

- Helpers: availability is measured bi-weekly. If you'll be unavailable for more than two weeks, notify [@Mods]({{ '/guides/moderation.html' | relative_url }}).
- NPCs: try to be available for at least one scene per month to be considered active.
- DMs: if you haven't run a game within two months, staff may check in about future plans.

### Inactivity and stepping down

- After a month of complete inactivity and no communication, Mods will attempt to contact you. No response may lead to removal from staff.
- If you need to step down temporarily, communicate with a Mod. Options include being kept as `@Staff` or stepping down fully.
- There is no punishment for asking to step down â€” communicate your needs.

### Leaving for emergencies

- If you must leave for an emergency, give as much notice as possible, notify Mods, and let any RP partners know.
